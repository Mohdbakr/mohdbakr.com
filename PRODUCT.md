# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and potential collaborators in data science and analytics. Secondary: the broader data science community who may encounter the site through the future blog. Visitors come to assess technical credibility, understand domain depth (internal audit, fraud, FP&A, risk), and get a sense of Mohamed as a full person — not just a resume. They arrive with moderate skepticism: they've seen generic portfolios before and can spot one instantly.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio and future blog for Mohamed Bakr — a data scientist with two decades of domain experience and hobbies that include Arabic calligraphy. The site exists to do two things simultaneously: establish analytical rigor and reveal a full, considered human being behind the work. Success means a visitor leaves both trusting the technical depth and feeling like they've encountered a distinctive, thoughtful person — not another interchangeable data science resume.

## Brand Personality

Detail-oriented. Precise. Experienced. High quality.

Voice: calm authority without arrogance. Formal enough to signal seriousness, warm enough to signal humanity.

Visual source of truth: `_brand.yml` (colors, typography, logo variants). All design decisions should align with the tokens defined there.

## Anti-references

- Overly AI-generated aesthetics: gradient blobs, glassmorphism everywhere, hero metrics with big numbers, identical card grids
- LinkedIn and corporate HR-portal styling: blues, sans-serif hierarchy, "results-driven professional" energy
- Template-generated portfolios: Bootstrap grid clones, GitHub Pages defaults, cookie-cutter developer portfolio structure
- Anything that screams "I generated this in 10 minutes" — no visual decisions that could belong to anyone

## Design Principles

1. **Every detail is intentional.** Nothing is arbitrary — spacing, type choices, color application, and copy all serve a purpose. If it can't be explained, remove it.
2. **Show the whole person.** Technical rigor and human warmth coexist on every page. One without the other is incomplete.
3. **Craft signals expertise.** The quality of the design is itself a proof point. A data scientist who builds a sloppy portfolio undermines their own credibility. A high-quality, precise one reinforces it.
4. **Accessible by default.** Every person with a disability should feel genuinely welcomed, not accommodated as an afterthought. Exceed WCAG AA where possible, target AAA on critical reading paths.
5. **Distinctive or nothing.** If a design choice could belong to any portfolio, it belongs to none. Every visual decision should be traceable back to Mohamed specifically.
